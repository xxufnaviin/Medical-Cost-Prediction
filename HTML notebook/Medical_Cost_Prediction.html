Problem Statement¶

Healthcare costs are rising globally, with individual medical insurance charges often varying widely due to factors like age, BMI, smoking status, and more. This unpredictability poses a challenge for insurance providers to accurately assess risk and set fair premiums. Inconsistent pricing can lead to undercharging high-risk individuals or overcharging low-risk ones, affecting both profitability and fairness. By analyzing historical insurance data and building predictive models, we can identify key cost drivers and forecast charges more accurately, leading to data-driven pricing, better resource planning, and improved customer satisfaction.
